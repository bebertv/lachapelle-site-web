History

The 19th Century Religious Querelle

When a priest of Lachapelle "preached the truths of the Gospel, not the errors of Rome"

The Abbé Edouard Bructe

En 1840, the abbotEdward Noise(born in Nancy in 1799, former military chaplain, knight of the Legion of Honour since 1838) was appointed parish priest of Lachapelle. Coming from Fontenay-aux-Roses where he taught philosophy, he brought with him his sister and his two nieces.

Fromfebruary 1842The bishopric of Montauban refused to pay certain ecclesiastical royalties at the instigation of his parish priest. Forbidden by Bishop Chaudru de Trélissac, Bruitte replied by refusing to submit and published the31 january 1843, in Lyon"Friendly in Rome"- five editions in two years, - judicial information for insulting religion.

He abjured, convolved in righteous marriage, and was replaced at Lachapelle by a succession of unstable priests: Groussou (1842-1844), Glayel (1844-1847), then Lagrange (1847-1857), who finally brought stability.

The Protestant Community

En 1847, under the episcopate ofBishop Doney19 faithful of Lachapelle addressed a petition to the Consistory of Montauban. An oratory was opened on28 november 1847, served first by Pastor Frossard. The11 august 1850Peter Mommeja bought a house to make it a temple, consecrated the28 may 1851.

In 1856, Pastor Corbière29 faithfulLachapelle (13), Lavit (5), Castéra-Bouzet (5), Balignac (4), Gramont (2). But the financial situation remained fragile: deficits of 841 F (1855) and 653 F (1856), covered by subsidies from the evangelical societies of Nîmes, Geneva and Paris.

Pastor Corbière noted in 1856:"They did not grasp the Gospel on the best side. Above all, they accepted our religion by its negative points, by its opposition to Catholicism."A conversion by reaction, in short.
